Project Milestones

Note

The purpose of the milestones is to help you monitoring your progress throughout the semester.

In each milestone session, each project group briefly presents their current process and we can discuss open questions (approx. 2 min presentation + 5 min discussion per group).

For this purpose, each group is supposed to prepare 1–2 slides for each of the milestones and upload them on Ilias until midnight before the respective session.

Important

Check out the schedule for the actual dates.

Milestone 1: Research question

Goals to be reached:

  • Your research question and your hypotheses are clearly formulated.
  • You decided on how you want to test your hypotheses (experimental design, independent and dependent measures).

Notes: You can use the paper corresponding to your project as a starting point and either replicate their experiment or come up with your own research question based on the paper.

Slides: Prepare 1–2 Slides showing:

  • Teamname + Members
  • Reminder on general idea of the experiment
  • The main research question(s) (= your “spin” on the project)
  • What EyeTracking measure(s) does the research question(s) map to?
Tip

Research questions can often be answered by a “yes” or “no”.

For instance, your (an arguable trivial) research question could be:

“Does highlighting every other word make a text more readable”

with your Hypothesis/EyeTracking measure for example being:

  1. shorter fixation durations on the highlighted words
  2. shorter total reading time for highlight-enabled sentences
  3. less regressions in highlight-enabled sentences
Milestone 2: Experimental design, paradigm, stimuli

Goals to be reached:

  • Prepared the experimental paradigm, including what are your conditions, how will you randomize (you do not need to have a working implementation in OpenSesame already, but it can guide you).
  • Defined and prepared your stimuli.

Slides: Prepare 1–2 Slides showing:

  • The central research question (one sentence, very brief)

  • The experimental procedure, no need to make it beautiful, but could look like this:

  • Name all conditions and their levels. Also add whether they are between (group-design) or within subjects (each subject sees multiple levels of this condition).

condition levels within/between
Difficulty hard,easy,medium between-subjects
Contrast 0–100% within subjects
  • How will you do the balancing and randomization?
  • What are your stimuli? Provide some examples.
Milestone 3: Experiment

Goals to be reached:

  • Implement the full experiment:
  • Test the experiment in the lab - especially check that triggers are available!

Slides: No slides this time!

Hand in

  • Upload the full .osexp file to Ilias.
  • Upload a second, “demo”-version of your experiment:
    • Should take 1–2 minutes (reduce the number of trials, or break early) to run.
    • Should make showcase different conditions.
    • Should have advanced mouse-simulation activated.
Milestone 4: Pilot data

Goals to be reached:

  • Collect a pilot to find bugs/problems in your Milestone 3 experiment.
  • Conduct sanity-check data analyses.

Slides: Provide 1–2 slides with some sanity checks.

Sanity checks could be (depending on project, come up with your own!)

  • Plot time against x-position, adding vertical lines for each USER-log/trigger.
  • Plot x against y .
  • Count all logs/triggers and confirming all trials exist, and their temporal-distances are what’s to be expected.
  • Check for balancing/randomization errors.
  • …
  • Advanced: Calculate accuracy/precision while gaze is on a fixation-cross.
Milestone 5: Analysis pipeline

Goals to be reached:

  • Think about the minimal set of analyses needed to answer your research questions.
  • Draft the analysis pipeline and validate it using the data collected for Milestone 4.

Slides: Prepare 2–3 slides listing:

  • the research questions,
  • the analyses that are supposed to answer them,
  • expected results,
  • preliminary results based on pilot data from Milestone 4.
Important

If you have “blocker”-questions, please reach out to us. You can also ask in class or on Ilias.
